Overthinking is your enemy; make it your friend (Part 1)

December 8, 2020

Overthinking has always gotten a bad rap, and quite rightly so, because more often than not, it perpetuates thoughts that are not conducive to one’s psyche. I, for one, could attest to the fact that overthinking has made me take so many wrong decisions, mark incorrect answers in tests, interpret a situation in a way that turned out to be quite opposite of what I had imagined it to be, thinking about what other people are thinking (yeah, it’s cringeworthy), and the list goes on and on…

All this has made me question my own thinking process - it’s called metacognition, and yes, if you are thinking that it must be hard as touching your elbow with your tongue (which I suggest you try out), you are right. It’s a difficult process which requires one to actively look at their thoughts and emotions in an objective manner. So, while I am still learning about “thinking about thinking”, I could say that I have felt some changes, howsoever minute, in my daily life, like I feel I have more clarity, in a broader sense of the word. Few things that are aiding me in this process are mindfulness meditation: if you have a cluttered mind, it’s difficult to focus, and writing, of course!

I would elaborate more on these thoughts in some future post, but for now, I guess this is all I have to share.
